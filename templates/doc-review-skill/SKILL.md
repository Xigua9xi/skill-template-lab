# Document Review Skill

Use this template when reviewing, restructuring, summarizing, or transforming a document.

## When to use

- The source document is the authority.
- The output should preserve supported claims.
- The user needs concrete edits, findings, or a structured review.

## Inputs

Define accepted inputs here:

- Document path or pasted text.
- Review goal.
- Target audience.
- Required output format.
- Any style, compliance, or citation constraints.

## Workflow

1. Identify the document type and intended audience.
2. Extract or read the source content.
3. Preserve claims that are supported by the source.
4. Flag missing, ambiguous, or unsupported claims.
5. Produce concrete edits, findings, or a structured output.
6. State remaining uncertainty and any required human review.

## Verification

Choose checks appropriate to the task:

- All required sections are present.
- Findings cite source sections or excerpts.
- Unsupported claims are removed or marked.
- Output format matches the requested structure.

## Safety boundaries

- Do not fabricate facts or citations.
- Do not silently change the author's intent.
- Do not expose private document content beyond the requested output.
- Do not present review assistance as final legal, medical, financial, or compliance advice.
